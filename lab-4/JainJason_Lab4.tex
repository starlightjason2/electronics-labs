\section{Time-dependent analysis of active RL filters}

We measure the time constant of the RL circuit, first calculating the theoretical value, then comparing it to the experimental value. The resistance is $R=\qty{996}{\ohm}$ and the inductance is $L=\qty{23.1}{\milli\henry}$, giving us a theoretical time constant of $\tau = L/R = \qty{23.2}{\micro\second}$. Our experimental value is found by observing the time it takes for the inductor current to reach $1/e$ of its total value while discharging, and $1 - (1/e)$ of its total value while charging.

Using an op-amp based active filter configuration with inductors, we measure the output waveforms and compare them to the input signals. The low-pass active RL filter demonstrates integration behavior when presented with a square wave input at \qty{10}{\kilo\hertz}. The output voltage shows the characteristic exponential charging and discharging curves as the inductor responds to the input transitions.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{output/low_pass_square_wave_10kHz.png}
    \caption{Active RL low-pass filter response to \qty{10}{\kilo\hertz} square wave input, showing integration behavior with exponential charging and discharging.}
    \label{fig:low_pass_10kHz}
\end{figure}

The high-pass active RL filter exhibits differentiation behavior, producing voltage spikes at the transitions of the input square wave. This demonstrates the filter's ability to pass high-frequency components while blocking low-frequency signals.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{output/high_pass_square_wave_10kHz.png}
    \caption{Active RL high-pass filter response to \qty{10}{\kilo\hertz} square wave input, showing differentiation behavior with voltage spikes at transitions.}
    \label{fig:high_pass_10kHz}
\end{figure}

\section{Fourier analysis of filtered signals}

We perform Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) analysis on the output signals from the low-pass filter to examine their frequency content. The FFT reveals how the filter attenuates different frequency components of the input signal.

For a \qty{400}{\hertz} input signal, the FFT shows the fundamental frequency and its harmonics, with higher harmonics progressively attenuated by the low-pass filter characteristics.

\begin{figure}[H]

\section{Frequency response of active RL high-pass filter}

We characterize the frequency response of the active RL high-pass filter by measuring its gain and phase shift across a range of frequencies from \qty{1}{\hertz} to \qty{1}{\mega\hertz}. Using the time constant of $\tau=\qty{23.2}{\micro\second}$, we find the theoretical characteristic \textit{angular} frequency of $\omega_c=1 / \tau=\qty{43.1}{\kilo\hertz}$.

The Bode plot shows the characteristic high-pass behavior with a cutoff frequency where the gain is \qty{-3}{\decibel} below the passband level. Below the cutoff frequency, the gain decreases at approximately \qty{20}{\decibel} per decade.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{output/high_pass_bode_plot.png}
    \caption{Active high-pass filter Bode plot showing gain vs frequency. The cutoff frequency is marked where gain drops by \qty{-3}{\decibel}.}
    \label{fig:high_pass_bode}
\end{figure}

The phase response shows the characteristic \ang{45} phase shift at the cutoff frequency, transitioning from \ang{90} at low frequencies to \ang{0} at high frequencies.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{output/high_pass_phase_plot.png}
    \caption{Active high-pass filter phase plot showing phase shift vs frequency.}
    \label{fig:high_pass_phase}
\end{figure}
