\section{Op-amp circuits}

\subsection{Open-loop gain}

We set up an open-loop op-amp circuit. Table~\ref{tab:part1a} shows the measured voltages. At the critical positive voltage $V_+ = \qty{1.33}{\volt}$, the negative voltage jumps up from $V_- = \qty{0.7}{\volt}$ to $V_-=\qty{2}{\volt}$.

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{cc}
        \hline

        \hline
        0.5 & 0.7 \\
        1.3 & 0.7 \\
        1.33 & 2.0 \\
        3.3 & 2.0 \\
        \hline
    \end{tabular}
    \caption{Open-loop gain measurements.}
    \label{tab:part1a}
\end{table}

\subsection{Follower}

Figure~\ref{fig:part1b} shows the follower configuration data with a linear fit. The unity-gain buffer matches $v_-$ to $v_+$. The fitted slope is $m = 1.003$, a $0.3\%$ difference from the theoretical value of $m = 1$, confirming near-ideal unity-gain behavior.

\section{Part 2}

We build Circuit~\ref{fig:circuit} and record voltage across the diode as a function of current (Figure~\ref{fig:part2}). The fitted slope $m = \qty{-0.109}{\volt\per\milli\ampere}$ corresponds to a feedback resistance of $R_f = |1/m| = \qty{9.2}{\kilo\ohm}$, a $8\%$ difference from the nominal $\qty{10}{\kilo\ohm}$ potentiometer. The op-amp linearizes the diode's logarithmic $I$--$V$ characteristic into a linear relationship. 

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.5\textwidth]{output/part2-circuit.png}
    \caption{Closed-loop op-amp circuit for linearizing a diode.}

\end{figure}
